Uncovering how Bitcoin fear and greed shapes trading outcomes on Hyperliquid's perpetuals exchange
01 Data Ingestion & Merging Foundation ▾

Started with two raw datasets: 211,224 Hyperliquid trade rows (compressed .gz) and 2,644 days of Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index readings. Parsed custom IST timestamp format, normalised both datasets to date-grain, and performed a left join achieving 99.997% coverage.

  • 211,224 trade rows from 32 unique traders across 246 coins
  • 104,408 non-zero Closed PnL rows used for performance metrics
  • Sentiment spans Feb 2018 – May 2025; trade data is full-year 2024

04 Key Findings & Patterns Insight ▾

The analysis uncovered several non-obvious patterns that have direct strategy implications:

  • Contrarian accumulation: Traders deploy the most capital ($7,816 avg) during Fear — systematically buying when others panic
  • Distribution in euphoria: BUY ratio drops to 44.9% during Extreme Greed — traders sell into strength
  • Sentiment bifurcation: Fear = highest win rate (87.3%); Extreme Greed = highest dollar PnL ($130) — two distinct profit mechanisms
  • Seasonal overlay: Q1 months show elevated Fear-regime PnL; September/October see weakest Greed-regime returns
  • @107 token edge: A single non-mainstream asset generated $2.78M — 27% of top-5 coin PnL
  • Fee efficiency: Gross vs net PnL gap <2% — traders are operating at institutional efficiency
